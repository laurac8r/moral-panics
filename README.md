# It's Never New — The Recurring Shape of Moral Panics

A single-page visual explainer on how moral panics recur across history — the
same machinery aimed first at new media, and far more often at whole groups of
people.

**▶ Live site:** https://laurac8r.github.io/moral-panics/

> "Every generation thinks their moral panic is brand new. It never is."
> — Jessica Kellgren-Fozard, [*Why Anti-Trans Arguments Feel So Familiar*](https://youtu.be/mL8knXMmiEg?t=61)

## What's inside

A self-contained `index.html` (no build step, no dependencies, opens on
double-click) with three fact-checked timelines and a closing pattern card:

- **When the panic is about a thing** — media & youth scares, from Socrates on
  writing to comics, rock 'n' roll, video games, and smartphones.
- **When the panic is about a person — LGBTQIA2+ lives** — from the colonial
  suppression of Two-Spirit roles through the Lavender Scare, Section 28, the
  AIDS "gay plague," and the contemporary "bathroom bill" / "groomer" panic.
- **When the panic is about a person — racial, ethnic & religious minorities** —
  the blood libel, witch trials, anti-Catholic nativism, Chinese Exclusion, the
  "Black beast rapist" myth and Jim Crow, the *Protocols* and the Holocaust,
  Reefer Madness, Japanese American incarceration, the "superpredator" myth, and
  the post-9/11 anti-Muslim panic.
- **The recurring template** — the four beats every panic shares.

The page opens with a **content warning**: it documents real persecution and
violence (genocide, lynching, the Holocaust, and more), and several of these
panics are ongoing.

## Accuracy

Every dated claim — names, laws, figures — was researched and adversarially
fact-checked against standard scholarship and primary records before inclusion.
Where figures vary by source, the most defensible documented number was used. A
**Sources & further reading** section at the bottom of the page lists the key
references (Stanley Cohen, the USHMM Holocaust Encyclopedia, the Equal Justice
Initiative, Densho, Canada's Truth and Reconciliation Commission, and others).

## Accessibility

Built to be readable, printable, and screen-reader friendly: semantic landmarks,
one `<h1>` with ordered headings, color contrast ≥ 4.5:1 in both light and dark
modes, visible focus rings, reduced-motion support, and a print stylesheet.
Verified at **Lighthouse accessibility 100/100**.
